Deficit


Deficit is a Semi-Fictional Experiential ADHD Story, based on real-life ADHD experiences. Navigate your way through several vignettes as you learn more about how structural barriers and attitudes can create a difficult environment for those whose brains work differently.

Created with: Twine (Harlowe), Google Docs, Photoshop, lots and lots of scrap paper.

Creation time: Approximately 9 months, released October 2020.


What's this about?

Deficit began as an experiment in format: to test the waters of Interactive Fiction, and to explore storytelling in a medium with a low bar to entry. Twine provides an easy way to quickly iterate on a story, but with enough complexity to support branching dialogue. I was inspired by games like Depression Quest and Nonbinary, and encouraged to start writing Deficit after describing in a tweet having struggled through a conference where I just could not pay attention. Subsequently, I engaged with First Person Scholar to eventually publish my work, and who gave me excellent feedback as I made the game. You can read my Artist's Statement on FPS here.

The game is structured as a series of vignettes; in it, you will explore several scenes inspired by real-life experiences. I believe that powerful stories can be driven by gameplay, and as such, the experiences in the game are bite-sized, punchy, and varied— much like how my own attention span works.


One of the most rewarding experiences in making Deficit has been in the responses to it. Numerous people have left comments and given feedback about how it made them feel.

I've had people message me that playing it finally made them seek a diagnosis, or understand others or themselves better. A high school teacher in the US emailed me to ask if I could talk about the game and answer questions for their students. I feel humbled by how many people have shared and played my game— over 1400 as of April 2021.


Design Intentions

I wanted Deficit to be an accessible way for anyone (including non-gamers) to experience a slice of what ADHD life is like for some people.
